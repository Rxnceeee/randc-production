Document Transactions & Processing

Once engaged for a document processing service, a transaction record is created and its progress is tracked through the following status stages:

Pending → Submitted → Ongoing → Completed → To Claim → Claimed
Status Description
PendingTransaction has been initiated and is waiting to be formally submitted for processing.
SubmittedDocument has been submitted and is waiting to be picked up by processing staff.
OngoingDocument is currently being processed by RandC staff at government agencies.
CompletedDocument processing is complete and the output document is ready.
To ClaimDocument is ready and available for the client to physically claim at the office.
ClaimedDocument has been successfully claimed by the client. The process is finished.
CancelledThe transaction was cancelled before completion.

Clients may view a full timeline of status changes, staff remarks, and any attached photos through the Transaction Timeline feature in the client portal. Processing times vary depending on government agency workload, document complexity, and compliance requirements, and are provided as estimates only.

RandC is not responsible for delays caused by third-party government agencies, incomplete client-provided documents, or force majeure events.
⏰ Ready-to-Claim Deadline & Penalty Policy
Documents marked as To Claim must be physically claimed at the RandC office within 7 calendar days from the date the document was updated to To Claim .

Failure to claim within 7-days will have a ₱200.00 penalty fee, which must be settled prior to or upon claiming the document. Clients will be notified by email and in-portal notification when their document is ready, including the exact claim deadline and penalty warning.

Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as your account is active and for a reasonable period afterward as required by law or business necessity. Specifically:

  • Active account data is retained for the duration of your account's existence.
  • Transaction records are retained for a minimum of 5 years from the date of completion to comply with Philippine property law requirements.
  • Chat messages are retained for 2 years from the date of the conversation.

  • Upon account deletion at your request, personal identity and contact data will be anonymized within 30 days, except where retention is legally required.

Privacy Policy

Your Rights Under Philippine Law

As a data subject under the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173), you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to be Informed: You have the right to know what data we collect and how we use it — as described in this policy.
  • Right to Access: You may request a copy of the personal data we hold about you at any time.
  • Right to Rectification: You may correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data held by us.
  • Right to Erasure / Blocking: You may request deletion or blocking of your data in cases where it is no longer necessary for the purposes it was collected, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Right to Object: You may object to the processing of your data for specific purposes.
  • Right to Data Portability: You may request a structured, machine-readable copy of data you provided to us.
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: You may file a complaint with the National Privacy Commission (NPC) if you believe your rights have been violated.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer using the contact details in Section 17.

Data Security

RandC implements reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These include:

  • Password hashing using industry-standard algorithms — plaintext passwords are never stored.
  • Token-based authentication (JWT) for secure session management.
  • Email verification required for account activation, preventing unauthorized registrations.
  • Role-based access controls ensuring that client data is only accessible to authorized staff.
  • Encrypted transmission of data via HTTPS.
While we take reasonable precautions, no system is completely immune to security threats. In the event of a data breach affecting your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and the National Privacy Commission within 72 hours as required by the NPC Circular on Personal Data Breach Management.

Privacy Policy

Data Privacy Act Compliance

RandC is committed to full compliance with the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (R.A. 10173) and its Implementing Rules and Regulations, as enforced by the National Privacy Commission of the Philippines.

This includes adherence to the three core principles of data processing under Philippine law:

  • Transparency: We inform you of all data collection and processing activities through this Privacy Policy.
  • Legitimate Purpose: All data processing is tied to a specific, declared, and lawful purpose directly connected to providing our document processing services.
  • Proportionality: We collect only the minimum data necessary to fulfill the stated purposes and do not use it in ways incompatible with those purposes.

Our designated Data Protection Officer (DPO) oversees compliance with the DPA and handles data subject rights requests. Contact details are provided in Section 17.
